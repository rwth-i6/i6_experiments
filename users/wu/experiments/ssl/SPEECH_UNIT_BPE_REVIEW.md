# BPE-Style and Bottom-Up Unit Discovery on Speech - Literature Review

*Date: 2026-06-20. Scope: work that DISCOVERS speech units in a BPE-like way — literal acoustic BPE on discrete tokens, bottom-up greedy/agglomerative merge on speech, and the MDL/compression-driven discovery the BPE algorithm approximates — assessed strictly against OUR setup (frozen BEST-RQ layer-9 @ 25 Hz / 40 ms, a TRAINABLE CIF segmenter producing deduped ~128-code "syllable-ish" sequences, second-level Conformer + masked prediction vs a frozen 128-kmeans codebook; downstream CTC-FT sanity = 6.46/14.33). Companion to LITERATURE_REVIEW.md (text-prior injection), which concluded the binding constraint is our TARGET, not the segmenter, and that a light text prior is necessary-but-not-sufficient.*

## 0. TL;DR and fit to us

There IS a rich, multi-sense literature for "BPE on speech," and it splits cleanly into a **cheap-but-shallow** branch (literal frequency-merge: acoustic BPE, acoustic pieces, unigram-LM on units) and a **principled** branch (acoustic-similarity / self-distillation merge: SyllableLM, Sylber, ES-KMeans, DPDP). The verdict converges hard with LITERATURE_REVIEW.md:

- **Literal acoustic BPE is a length/compute-compression tool, not a quality tool.** It is purely frequency-driven and permutation-invariant over code identity, so — exactly like the marginal/inventory-matching priors in the companion review — it **cannot by itself make unit↔text mapping easier**. The same identifiability point applies: frequency merging is blind to linguistic identity.
- **The single most decisive datapoint:** every syllabic paper that benchmarked literal BPE against learned units found BPE buys compression but LOSES on quality at matched rate — SyllableLM's own BPE-on-TWIST baseline scores sWUGGY 61.8 / sBLIMP 54.5 vs 72.2 / 62.4 for loss-derived syllabic units; Cuervo & Marxer (2024) found Unigram-on-HuBERT **scaled WORSE** downstream; SpiRit-LM and SUTLM both decline to BPE the speech-unit stream.
- **On noisy units the math actively flips against us.** Dekel & Fernandez's own (1-ε)^n accounting shows BPE RAISES per-token error and nets a win only when the base stream is near-deterministic (ε≈0.1%). Our deduped codes are demonstrably impure (114-125 active of 128, +0.9 bits transition entropy vs phonemes), so the precondition for a net win is likely **violated**; a single sub-unit error then corrupts a whole merged token.
- **Our CIF segmenter already IS the better lever.** The field's answer to "coarsen into syllable/word-like units with text-like statistics" is a learned structured tokenizer (Sylber/SyllableLM), which is the acoustic-similarity-merge family our CIF already belongs to. The missing piece is the TARGET (frozen-mean + 128-kmeans), not the variable-length mechanism.

**Net:** Do the literal-BPE experiment because it is near-free and informative for the speech-LLM compression budget — but treat it as a FINISHING layer downstream of fixing the target, sweep vocab small (hundreds, given ~120 base codes), prefer unigram-LM/BPE-dropout over greedy BPE, and judge it ONLY on vocab-invariant mappability (CER with a fixed/oracle mapper, MFA-oracle ceiling), **never** on perplexity. For the unsup-ASR goal specifically, acoustic BPE adds nothing to identifiability — it is downstream of, not a substitute for, the fix-target-first / text-prior program in the companion review.

## 1. Framing: the three senses of "BPE on speech"

There are three distinct things people mean by "discover units BPE-style," and they matter for our two goals very differently.

**(i) Literal BPE / unigram-LM / SentencePiece on discrete tokens ("acoustic BPE").** Map each discrete unit id to a private-use Unicode char, run off-the-shelf SentencePiece, re-encode. The merge criterion is pure corpus pair-frequency — nothing acoustic or linguistic enters. This is the cheapest thing we could run on our deduped CIF codes today. It is **lossless and strictly more powerful than run-length dedup** (it merges non-identical recurring n-grams, not just repeats), but on an ALREADY-deduped stream that extra power is small, and it is permutation-invariant — so it speaks ONLY to **Goal #2 (speech-LLM tokenizer / sequence compression)** and contributes nothing to **Goal #1 (unsup-ASR mappability)** by itself. This is the same conclusion the companion review reached for marginal/inventory matching: it cannot break the permutation-identifiability symmetry that PUSM skipgram/positional priors address.

**(ii) Bottom-up greedy MERGE directly on speech (continuous or discrete) by acoustic affinity.** Sylber's cosine-similarity merge of adjacent frames, SyllableLM's loss-min-cut boundaries, ES-KMeans / BES-GMM joint DP-resegmentation + clustering, DPDP's duration-penalized DP. Here the "merge basis" is *signal structure* (within-segment feature distance), not corpus frequency. This family grows units that respect linguistic boundaries and is the one that actually moves unit↔text mappability — and **our CIF integrate-and-fire is already a member of it.**

**(iii) MDL / compression-driven / information-theoretic discovery** — the principle BPE crudely approximates. BES-GMM's Bayesian marginal likelihood, explicit description-length unit induction, adaptor grammars, branching-entropy boundary cues. These give the right *objective* (bits to code the lexicon + bits to code the data) and the right *diagnostics* (branching entropy — the same toolkit that produced our "+0.9 bits transition entropy vs phonemes" number), but on noisy 128-ish codes they overfit noise; they are evaluation lenses and post-target-fix candidates, not today's engine.

Mapping to our goals: sense (i) → Goal #2 only, cheap; sense (ii) → both goals, this is where the payoff is and where CIF already sits; sense (iii) → the yardstick + a future second stage once codes are clean.

## 2. Acoustic / speech BPE on discrete tokens

The mechanism is identical everywhere: id→Unicode char→SentencePiece BPE/Unigram. Reported wins are real but narrow: **sequence-length compression (1.6-2.4×) and AR-decoder speed/stability** (Shen et al.; Li et al.; Dekel & Fernandez formalize the (1-ε)^n exposure-bias argument). The decisive counter-evidence is that Cuervo & Marxer found Unigram-on-HuBERT scales WORSE downstream, and the leading speech-text LMs (SpiRit-LM, SUTLM) deliberately DON'T BPE the speech-unit stream — they dedup speech and BPE only text. Note the perverse property reported in the review line (Guo et al. 2025): aBPE's compression gain is **negatively correlated with token information density** — it compresses hardest where there is least linguistic content (silence, long vowels), i.e. compression ≠ quality. And the evaluation guardrail (arXiv:2601.06329): aBPE "wins" are usually per-token PPL or syntax-accuracy on the BPE'd vocabulary, which is not comparable across vocabularies — a trap we must avoid.

Crucially for us: most headline 1.6-5× compression is measured against raw 25-75 Hz **un-deduped** streams (~2500 frames). Our codes are already deduped at ~phoneme length-ratio 1.0, so we live in the regime with **far smaller compression headroom**, and we lose the option (Chang 2023) of BPE-on-undeduped, which tends to beat dedup-then-BPE.

## 3. Discrete units + subword tokenization for ASR / ST / S2ST

The standard pipeline — dedup (run-length collapse) then BPE/SentencePiece on SSL k-means tokens → encoder-decoder — yields two crisp empirical laws with **opposite signs by task**: (1) for ASR/recognition, dedup ALONE slightly HURTS WER (it deletes duration/repeat cues: Chang 2023, 5.9/12.8 → 6.4/13.2), and BPE-on-units is roughly WER-neutral-to-+6%rel — the win is length/storage, not accuracy; (2) for generation/translation (S2ST/ST), dedup + subword HELPS because shorter targets ease AR decoding (Lee 2022, reduced 38.2 vs stacked 34.4 BLEU). Our goal spans both, and our CIF codes are **structurally already deduped**, committing us to the "recognition-hurts / generation-helps" regime. Two more load-bearing facts: the Interspeech-2024 DSU challenge leaderboard moved by improving the UNITS (richer SSL streams, better k-means), **not** by better subwording — independent corroboration that the binding constraint is the target; and Tsiamas (2024) explicitly warns BPE-on-DSU is **high-variance and non-monotone** (32K/len-118 ≈ 2 BLEU worse than 8K/len-150). Every strong result here leaned on supervision or a second signal — there is **no** precedent of dedup+BPE alone enabling unsupervised text mapping. The one method targeting mappability rather than compression is Ren 2022 "acoustic piece" (BPE-merged units used as the SSL prediction TARGET), which points the same way as fix-target-first.

## 4. Bottom-up / agglomerative / MDL unit & word discovery

This is the family that does what BPE *approximates* but with an **acoustic-similarity or MDL** merge basis instead of frequency — and it is where our CIF segmenter already lives. ES-KMeans / BES-GMM jointly optimize segmentation + clustering under within-cluster variance (a Gaussian-MDL surrogate); our CIF alpha-weighted convex segment means ARE exactly ES-KMeans-style acoustic word embeddings, so an ES-KMeans-style DP-resegmentation + k-means could grow word-like units on top of CIF with a principled criterion rather than frequency BPE. DPDP's duration-penalized DP (encoding cost + λ·duration) is the discrete analogue of our CIF quantity loss — a clean rate knob without GAN/Bayesian machinery. The most important honest caveat in this whole angle comes from Visser/van Niekerk/Kamper 2025 (DPDP-for-SLM): coarsening HELPS LM/resynthesis but can **HURT phone/word discrimination** — compression and mappability genuinely trade off, which must be our acceptance criterion. The strongest external corroboration of the companion review's verdict: Malan/van Niekerk/Kamper (2024-2025) and arXiv:2510.09225 ("limited by representations rather than clustering") both find the **TYPE-clustering / representation step is the bottleneck, not boundary placement or the merge algorithm** — a frequency-BPE coarsener on a non-discriminative 128-kmeans target inherits its weakness. And Wang/Ni/Chang 2025 (syllable-level unsup-ASR, MLM not GAN, up to 40% rel CER reduction) is the highest-relevance validation of Goal #1 — but it needs SyllableLM-grade units, setting the quality bar our coarsened CIF codes must clear.

## 5. Variable-length tokenizers for speech LLMs ("learned BPE")

These obtain variable length by boundary segmentation / dynamic chunking / adaptive clustering rather than frequency merges — and the literature here delivers the single most decisive evidence for our question, because several of these papers ran literal BPE as their *own baseline* and beat it. SyllableLM: BPE-on-deduped-TWIST best at 4k vocab gives sWUGGY 61.8 / sBLIMP 54.5 vs **72.2 / 62.4** for loss-derived syllabic units at comparable rate. Sylber: BPE-on-HuBERT cuts tokens but with large info loss (WER 13.32) and lower coding-rate than the learned merge. The lesson is unanimous: **the SLM/interface gains come from RE-DERIVING units from the model's loss/feature landscape, not from frequency-merging an existing stream** — so coarsening our deduped CIF codes with BPE would likely reproduce their *weak BPE* result, not their *strong syllable* result. Two reusable engineering tricks for our interface: VARSTok folds span into the token id (implicit duration coding) and DyCAST predicts duration with a negative-binomial head — both let a downstream speech LLM consume **one token per CIF segment with its duration encoded**. DyCAST (char-aligned) and SpiRit-LM (interleaving) require paired text and are off-limits for unsup-ASR, but quantify the prize a light text prior could buy (and SpiRit-LM is the contrast case: BPE the text, leave speech fixed — the exact gap our CIF targets).

## 6. When it fails: negative results & the noisy-unit caveat

The adversarial picture is sharp and directly aimed at our regime:

- **Precondition asymmetry.** Every positive acoustic-BPE result is on CLEAN, already-discriminative units (synthesized DAUs ε≈0.1%, well-tuned HuBERT-2048, or a supervised fine-tune downstream). There is **no published evaluation of acoustic BPE on deliberately noisy/impure units** — exactly our regime (114-125 active codes, +0.9 bits transition entropy vs phonemes). The literature's silence here is itself a negative result.
- **Error amplification is real and quantified.** Dekel & Fernandez's (1-ε)^n model shows BPE RAISES per-token error (0.097→0.14%, ~45% rel) and nets a win only when length shrinks faster — i.e. only on a near-deterministic base. On noisy units the arithmetic predicts a **crossover** where a longer reliable sequence BEATS a shorter sequence of unreliable merged tokens, because each merge folds several uncertain decisions into one all-or-nothing token and a single sub-unit error corrupts the whole merged token.
- **Conceptual mismatch confirmed concretely.** Text BPE assumes a stable, deterministic, discrete symbol per position; SSL units do not provide that — StableToken reports ~16-26% of tokens flip under benign acoustic perturbation, and clusters even encode the recording/noise condition (arXiv:2409.02565). BPE merges are a deterministic function of an UNSTABLE input, so the same utterance from a different speaker yields a different unit string → different greedy merges → different super-tokens, breaking cross-speaker merge consistency (a vocab learned on LS-960 may mis-segment elsewhere). Our CIF rides on frozen BEST-RQ features that are themselves speaker/recording dependent, so BPE bakes that variability into the merge table.
- **Configuration brittleness on a small alphabet.** Li et al. (2024) document a degenerate repeated-token failure loop and an actual WER **regression** (8192-centroids + BPE-10k = 8.0 vs 7.8 no-BPE) when the #base-codes-to-vocab ratio is bad. Our ~120-code alphabet sits squarely in that risky small-alphabet regime.
- **Merges ignore linguistic structure.** Frequency merges are dominated by silence / co-articulation / long-vowel repetition, not morphemes/syllables; the review line's "gain negatively correlated with information density" means BPE compresses hardest where there is least content. This is the **same permutation-identifiability gap** the companion review flagged: frequency ≠ mappability.
- **Root cause is upstream.** arXiv:2510.09225 ("limited by representations rather than clustering") and the Kamper boundary-vs-clustering analyses show bottom-up grouping **cannot beat the representation ceiling**. BPE re-bins existing (im)purity; it cannot manufacture discriminativeness the base codes lack.
- **Metric trap.** Many aBPE "wins" are per-token / length-normalized PPL, not comparable across vocabularies (arXiv:2601.06329). Do not let a compression-driven PPL drop masquerade as quality.

## 7. Fit to our project: concrete cheap experiments

Ranked by effort-vs-payoff. Steps 1-3 are this-week; the real payoff is steps 5-6.

1. **Baseline diagnostics on current deduped CIF codes (near-zero cost, do FIRST).** Compute branching-entropy / description-length, base per-token reliability/purity (PNMI, code→phone confusion to test the ε precondition), and same-word-different-speaker code-string agreement (to test merge consistency). These gate everything: if per-token reliability is low, the (1-ε)^n math says BPE will hurt. Effort: trivial. Payoff: decides whether step 2 is even worth interpreting positively.

2. **Literal unigram-LM coarsening sweep on deduped CIF codes (near-zero cost).** Run SentencePiece **unigram-LM** (not greedy BPE — probabilistic, robust to soft units; or BPE-dropout) at small vocab (1k/2k/4k given ~120 base codes). Report **bitrate + CER-with-fixed/oracle-mapper + MFA-oracle ceiling**, NEVER perplexity. Effort: trivial. Payoff: quantifies the ceiling of the cheap branch for the speech-LLM compression budget; expect modest compression (already deduped) and likely NO mappability gain. Keep vocab small — length→quality is non-monotone (Tsiamas).

3. **Joint speech-BPE + text-BPE for comparable inventories (cheap; the text-prior connection).** Per LITERATURE_REVIEW.md's text-prior bet: BPE the unit stream AND the text to comparable subword inventory sizes/rates so the marginals/inventories are matched. This is the natural BPE-side instantiation of the rate+inventory prior. **Honest caveat:** by the companion review's own argument this matching is **permutation-invariant and necessary-but-not-sufficient** — it does not break identifiability on its own (that needs PUSM skipgram/positional). So do this as the prep step for the prior, not as a standalone fix.

4. **Bitrate + CER reporting in the Interspeech-2024 DSU framing (cheap, presentational).** Report our coarsened CIF codes as (bitrate, CER) to place them on the field's compression-vs-accuracy axis. Effort: low. Payoff: defensible eval frame; clarifies we're improving units, not tokenizer.

5. **ES-KMeans / DPDP-style merge on CIF segment embeddings (moderate cost, real payoff).** Our CIF alpha-weighted convex means ARE ES-KMeans acoustic word embeddings; run joint DP-resegmentation + k-means (variance/MDL criterion) or DPDP duration-penalized DP to grow word-like units by **acoustic affinity, not frequency**. This is "smart BPE" — the principled-branch member our CIF already belongs to. Gate with the DPDP-2025 acceptance criterion: confirm phone/word discriminability did not drop.

6. **Fix the TARGET via SylBoost/Sylber-style self-distillation (highest cost, highest payoff — the real fix).** Replace frozen-mean + 128-kmeans with SylBoost EMA self-distillation / SyllableLM LossPred boundaries on frozen BEST-RQ L9, OR adopt Ren-2022 "acoustic piece" (BPE-merged codes as the masked-prediction TARGET, sense (i) used as a learning signal not a relabel). This is the same "fix-target-first" verdict as the companion review and the only step that moves unit↔text mappability. **Pre-gate with our seg-diag probe:** verify the BEST-RQ L9 masked-prediction loss truly spikes at boundaries at 25 Hz before committing to LossPred.

**What NOT to do:** Do not put acoustic BPE inside the SSL/CIF/segmentation loop or on the unsup-ASR mapping path — it is permutation-invariant, frequency-merges silence/co-articulation, and amplifies our +0.9-bit noise into a fragmented, speaker-contaminated vocabulary. Do not use greedy BPE on our small (~120) alphabet (repetition-collapse / WER-regression regime) — prefer unigram-LM/BPE-dropout. Do not justify any BPE-on-CIF result by perplexity. Do not expect BPE to substitute for fixing the target or for the text-prior program; it is a downstream compression FINISHING layer, ranked below both.
